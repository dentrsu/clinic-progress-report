Oracle Analytics — How It Works

A complete guide to the heuristic model behind progress scoring, velocity, forecasting, and risk detection in DentRSU Tracker.

DentRSU Tracker

What is the Oracle?

The Oracle is a rule-based analytics engine (heuristic model) that continuously monitors student treatment records and answers three questions automatically:

Progress Score

Where are you now? (0–100)

30-Day Velocity

How fast are you moving?

Est. Completion

When will you finish?

Risk Level

How urgent is attention needed?

Heuristic ≠ Machine Learning. The Oracle uses simplified, human-interpretable rules grounded in clinical domain knowledge. It does not train on historical data or improve over time. Think of it as an expert advisor following a structured checklist.
PART 1

Progress Score (0–100)

Measures the fraction of total required RSU + CDA units that have been verified by an instructor.

-- Formula progress_score = ROUND(verified_completion_pct × 100) verified_completion_pct = SUM(rsu_units + cda_units WHERE status = 'verified') ÷ SUM(minimum_rsu + minimum_cda FROM requirement_list)

Why only "verified" records?

Only instructor-approved records count. Pending and completed records are excluded because they haven't been officially confirmed — this gives a conservative, auditable number that reflects true locked-in progress.

Known Limitation: The denominator uses all requirements in the system, not just those for the student's current division. Students focusing on one division will see a lower score than their actual division-specific completion rate.
PART 2

Verified Velocity (30-Day)

Measures how many RSU units were officially verified within the last 4 weeks and 8 weeks.

-- 4-week velocity (used as the displayed "30-Day" figure) v_verified_4w = COALESCE(SUM(rsu_units), 0) WHERE status = 'verified' AND verified_at >= NOW() - interval '4 weeks' -- 8-week velocity (used for forecast stability) v_verified_8w = COALESCE(SUM(rsu_units), 0) WHERE status = 'verified' AND verified_at >= NOW() - interval '8 weeks'

Why verified_at — not created_at?

A student may submit a record (create it) months before it gets verified. Using creation time would attribute progress to the past, making velocity appear zero even when recent approvals just happened. Verification time correctly marks the moment progress was officially confirmed.

Known Limitation: Velocity tracks RSU units only — CDA units are excluded. Students in CDA-heavy divisions will see a lower velocity figure than their actual clinical output warrants.
Two windows explained: 4 weeks is sensitive — it reflects what's happening right now. 8 weeks is stable — it smooths out brief bursts. Both are measured and the best is used in the forecast (see Part 3).
PART 3

Estimated Completion Date (Forecast)

The Oracle asks: "If you keep moving at your current verified speed, when will you reach 100%?"

Step-by-step calculation

1
Pick the best available monthly velocity

Use the 8-week average (÷2 for monthly rate) or the 4-week rate — whichever is larger. This prevents the forecast collapsing to zero if recent weeks were slow.

2
Calculate remaining work as a percentage

remaining = 1 − verified_completion_pct

3
Estimate months to finish

months = CEIL( (remaining × 100) ÷ monthly_velocity )

The ×100 converts the velocity (raw RSU) to a "percentage-points per month" scale.

4
Project the calendar date

forecast = date_trunc('month', TODAY + months MONTHS)

-- Full formula (SQL) v_monthly_velocity := GREATEST(v_verified_8w / 2.0, -- annualised 8w avg v_verified_4w, -- direct 4w rate 0); -- floor at zero IF v_monthly_velocity > 0 AND v_verified_pct < 1 THEN v_months_remaining := CEIL((1 - v_verified_pct) * 100 / v_monthly_velocity); v_forecast_month := date_trunc('month', TODAY + v_months_remaining months); END IF;

When "Analyzing..." appears: If no records have been verified in the last 4 or 8 weeks, monthly velocity is 0 and no forecast can be generated. Projecting with zero velocity would give infinity, which is meaningless — so the system stays silent until new data arrives.
Linear Projection Warning: The model assumes velocity stays constant. Real-world bursts, pauses, illness, or exam periods are not accounted for. The forecast is a planning aid, not a guarantee.
PART 4

Risk Level (Green / Yellow / Orange / Red)

A 0–100 penalty-based score. Each risk condition adds a fixed number of points. Higher score = higher risk.

Penalty Rules

Condition Penalty Why this matters
No activity ≥ 30 days +25 pts Early warning sign of falling behind schedule.
No activity ≥ 60 days +15 pts (+40 total) Prolonged inactivity — urgent intervention likely needed.
≥ 10 records pending verification +15 pts Large backlog means progress isn't being locked in.
Zero verified velocity (8-week) +25 pts No momentum — the forecast cannot even run.
Within 90 days of deadline +20 pts Deadline proximity amplifies all other risks.

Risk Level Thresholds

ScoreLevelMeaning
0 – 24 Green — On Track Progress is healthy. No significant risks detected.
25 – 49 Yellow — At Risk One factor needs attention. Check factors panel.
50 – 74 Orange — High Risk Multiple factors active. Instructor conversation recommended.
75 – 100 Red — Critical Immediate action required to avoid graduation delay.
Why additive penalties? Each risk factor is independent — multiple risks compound. A student who is inactive AND near graduation AND has zero velocity scores 25+20+25 = 70 points (Orange) from just those three conditions. This intentional escalation catches combined risk patterns that any single threshold would miss.
No Historical Memory: Risk level reflects the current moment only. A student who has been Red for 2 months but just had a burst of verifications will immediately show Green on the next refresh. The system does not track trends over time.
PART 5

PART 6

When to Trust It — and When to Be Cautious

✅ High Trust Scenarios

  • Active student with consistent monthly verifications
  • Student within 90 days of graduation deadline
  • Multiple verified records across different divisions

⚠️ Medium Trust — Use with Judgment

  • Student returning after a long pause (velocity will recover slowly)
  • 3–5 records total — too little data for a stable forecast
  • Burst activity just before a deadline (velocity will spike, then drop)

🔴 Low Trust — Consult Instructor

  • First 2 weeks with only 1 verified record
  • Student primarily in CDA-heavy divisions (velocity undercounted)
  • Student on approved leave, clinic closure, or exam period

📌 Important Reminder

  • "Red" does not mean a student will fail
  • "Green" does not guarantee on-time graduation
  • Use Oracle as a conversation starter, not a final judgment

Overall System Cautions

Individual circumstances are invisible to the model. Medical leave, clinic closures, public holidays, and exam periods are not factored in. The Oracle will show declining metrics during any pause regardless of reason.
Progress Score uses a system-wide denominator. If not all requirements have minimum RSU/CDA values set, the score may be inflated relative to the actual requirement load for a specific student.
All calculations refresh on every record change. There is no historical trend line, weighted average, or seasonal adjustment. Every refresh is a full recalculation from the current data.
Recalculation is instant and automatic. Any time a record is created, updated, or verified, the Oracle refreshes for that student automatically in the background.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does "Analyzing..." appear even though I have submitted records?

Only verified records contribute to velocity. If none of your submitted records have been approved by an instructor in the last 4–8 weeks, the Oracle has no verified velocity to work with and cannot compute a forecast. Ask your instructor to verify your pending records.

My velocity dropped after my instructor approved old records. Is that normal?

Yes. Velocity is based on verified_at — the date of approval. Old records approved recently count toward the current window, but only until they fall outside the 4/8-week window. Consistent submission and verification keeps velocity stable.

My score is 18% but I feel I've done much more work. Why?

The Progress Score only counts verified records, and the denominator includes all requirements in the system. Submit pending work for verification, and work across multiple requirements to improve the score.

The forecast date seems too far away. Can I change the model?

The forecast is driven entirely by your verified velocity. The fastest way to move the date closer is to get more records verified consistently each month. Administrators can adjust cohort deadlines, but the calculation model itself is fixed.

How often does the Oracle recalculate?

Automatically every time a treatment record is created, updated, deleted, or verified — no manual action required. The student dashboard always shows the most recent snapshot.
